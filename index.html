Personal Projects

Self-Driving Scooter

Draper

At Draper, I worked with a team of interns to develop a novel autonomous mobility scooter to use as a scalable test vehicle for the main car. My primary contribution was mechanical design and manufacturing; I handled conceptualization to CAD design of the entire system, including remodeling the storebought chassis, remodeling many electronics, the mounts, extended chassis, and assembly of every component. I practiced electronics packaging design through consideration of sensor location preferences, constraints on noise interference between components, and mounting processes. I designed for manufacturing and designed for assembly, creating technical drawings of over a dozen mounts and parts that were professionally machined and 3D printed. In addition, I researched and coded base-level Lattice and RRT path planning algorithms from scratch in MATLAB, for use in comparison testing to improve efficiency and safety of path planning.

Solar System Yo-Yo

2.008 Design and Manufacturing II

With a team of 6 other students, we designed and manufactured many solar system themed yo-yos for the 2.008 (Design and Manufacturing II) project. The yo-yo consists of multiple injection molded and thermoformed parts to create the sun, planet, orbital tracks, body, and stand. DIY ball switches and an LED are also inside to light up the sun during centripetal motion. I designed and manufactured multi-cavity molds to injection mold the planets, adjusting design and process parameters to account for shrinkage and potential defects.

Linear Slide Robot

2.007 Design and Manufacturing I

In 2.007 (Design and Manufacturing I), I designed a robot in Solidworks with a custom linear slide for the purpose of lifting objects in competition. I manufactured the linear slide as well as built a "mini-me" robot.

Printed Hydraulics

MIT Distributed Robotics Lab (CSAIL)

In conjunction with Dr. MacCurdy of the MIT Distributed Robotics Lab, I contributed to the development of the iconic hexapod robot, demonstrating printed hydraulics and the fields' feasibility. My main contributions include design of the motor mount, as well as system design for a hydraulic control module which prescribes variable pressures to every point of actuation.

Anklebot

MIT Newman Lab (Biomechanics and Human Rehabilitation)

With the MIT Newman Lab for Biomechanics and Human Rehabilitation, I developed a GUI with real time plotting, calibration, and active stiffness and damping adjustment to improve experimental data logging for a wearable physical therapy device. The coding was done in Tcl (Tool Command Language).

Bertha

FIRST Tech Challenge (Team 6112)

With a team of three of my peers, I took on the FIRST Tech Challenge robotics competition for two years. As team lead, I spearheaded mechanical design, engineering documentation, sponsorship relations, and outreach efforts. My significant technical contributions include overall system design, and the development of our grappling hook pulley system to lift our 30 pound robot. In addition, I volunteered at local FIRST Lego League competitions for grades 4-8 as an emcee and referee to pay it forward.

Achievements: 1st Place Alliance (State), 2nd Place Inspire Award (State), 1st Place Inspire Award (Region), Think Award (Region), Motivate Award (State), 2x Motivate Award (District)

DIY Flashlight

2.670 Mechanical Engineering Tools

In the 2.670 Mechanical Engineering Tools class at MIT, I turned basic stock parts into a working flashlight. An internal AAA battery, lightbulb, and aluminum casing complete a circuit when joined together, shining behind a protective acrylic lens. To make this, I utilized and received formal training in various shop tools including the lathe, CNC mill, drill press, and tap and die set.
