Search and Rescue | 2.009 Senior Capstone

Brochure

For my 2.009 Senior Capstone, I delivered an alpha prototype of 10 total devices (3 unique designs) within 6 weeks as a leading designer among 18 engineering teammates, after 3 rapid iterations of mechanical enclosures. Together, the system of devices optimizes search and rescue missions by providing real-time information and visualization, and these specialized devices map to the existing search team architecture for easy integration. See the brochure above for product details.

I drove electronics integration: I delivered multiple battery compartments as well as a novel surface rechargeable battery pack and compartment that accepts both AAs as well as this pack, to allow for on-the-go replacement as well as an integrated solution. I also designed tablet mounting and button interfaces. I owned waterproofing design to reach an IP67 standard (pending), through custom gaskets and adhesives. I created toolpaths for CNC milling using HSMWorks and supported rubber molding processes.

To finish, I presented on behalf of the team at the class product launch to over 250,000 unique viewers and 1,100 live.

Assistive Robot Arm

2.12 Term Project

For my 2.12 robotics term project, my team of 10 delivered a serial elastic actuated robot to safely help hemiplegic patients with household tasks. I owned design of the arm linkage subsystem, including the lightweight structure, low friction bearing and thrust bearing joints, and timing belt power transmission to reduce cantilevered weight.

Mobile Testing Bench

Aperia Technologies

At Aperia Technologies, I owned development of a mobile cart for testing and prototyping of new systems or components. For example, the combination of a pneumatic manifold and flow meter, terminal blocks, and a threaded bolt pattern allows easy leakage testing of a membrane. I designed every aspect of the cart, including CAD, component selection, and integration of all pneumatic, electrical, and mechanical systems. I practiced design for optimization in cost, reliability, functionality, and more -- challenging every decision along the way, such as coarse versus fine threaded screws, steel versus aluminum, thickness based deflection calculations, switching versus linear power supplies, compression versus push to connect fittings, and more. I presented at multiple design reviews with senior level engineers and practiced communicating my design decisions and implementing feedback. This was a six-week project I completed in addition to my product management duties.

Novel Fluidic Actuator "Muscle" Test Rig

Vecna

At Vecna, I designed and implemented a test rig to evaluate a novel hydraulic actuator for use in a DARPA funded robot arm project. From initial CAD design to final assembly and construction of the individual muscles, the test rig was successful, lifting 200 pounds. (See Video!) Through this project, I practiced mechanism design and bolstered my understanding of hydraulics and hydraulic system construction.

Self-Driving Scooter

Draper

At Draper, I worked with a team of interns to develop a novel autonomous mobility scooter to use as a scalable test vehicle for the main car. My primary contribution was mechanical design and manufacturing; I handled conceptualization to CAD design of the entire system, including remodeling the storebought chassis, remodeling many electronics, the mounts, extended chassis, and assembly of every component. I practiced electronics packaging design through consideration of sensor location preferences, constraints on noise interference between components, and mounting processes. I designed for manufacturing and designed for assembly, creating technical drawings of over a dozen mounts and parts that were professionally machined and 3D printed. In addition, I researched and coded base-level Lattice and RRT path planning algorithms from scratch in MATLAB, for use in comparison testing to improve efficiency and safety of path planning.

Anklebot

MIT Newman Lab (Biomechanics and Human Rehabilitation)

With the MIT Newman Lab for Biomechanics and Human Rehabilitation, I developed a GUI with real time plotting, calibration, and active stiffness and damping adjustment to improve experimental data logging for a wearable physical therapy device. The coding was done in Tcl (Tool Command Language).

DIY Flashlight

2.670 Mechanical Engineering Tools

In the 2.670 Mechanical Engineering Tools class at MIT, I turned basic stock parts into a working flashlight. An internal AAA battery, lightbulb, and aluminum casing complete a circuit when joined together, shining behind a protective acrylic lens. To make this, I utilized and received formal training in various shop tools including the lathe, CNC mill, drill press, and tap and die set.
